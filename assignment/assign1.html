Biography: Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Early Life

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on October 31, 1875, in Nadiad, Gujarat. He came from a humble farming family. From a young age, he showed determination and courage. Despite limited resources, he pursued his education and later became a successful barrister.

He studied law in England and returned to India as a respected lawyer. But his heart was always inclined toward India's freedom movement.

Freedom Struggle and Leadership

Sardar Patel joined the Indian National Congress and became a close associate of Mahatma Gandhi. He led several movements like the Bardoli Satyagraha in 1928, where he earned the title “Sardar” (leader).

He played a vital role in India's freedom struggle by organizing farmers, leading protests, and advocating for non-violence. His discipline and leadership were admired across the nation.

Role in Post-Independence India

After independence, Sardar Patel became the first Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister of India. His biggest achievement was the integration of 562 princely states into the Indian Union. Because of this, he is called the "Iron Man of India".

He worked tirelessly to unite India and strengthen its administrative structure. His vision laid the foundation of a strong and united India.

Legacy

Sardar Patel passed away on December 15, 1950. In his honor, the Statue of Unity, the world’s tallest statue, was inaugurated in 2018 in Gujarat. It symbolizes his contribution and strong leadership.
